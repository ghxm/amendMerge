REPORT on the proposal for a European Parliament and Council decision amending Decision No 1254/96/EC laying down a series of guidelines for trans-European energy networks (COM(98)0542 - C4-0556/98 - 98/0284(COD))

25 February 1999

Committee on Research, Technological Development and Energy
Rapporteur: Gordon Adam

  • By letter of 30 September 1998 the Commission submitted to Parliament, pursuant to Article 129d(1) and 189b(2) of the EC Treaty, the proposal for a European Parliament and Council decision amending Decision No 1254/96/EC laying down a series of guidelines for trans-European energy networks.
  • A LEGISLATIVE PROPOSAL - DRAFT LEGISLATIVE RESOLUTION
  • B EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

By letter of 30 September 1998 the Commission submitted to Parliament, pursuant to Article 129d(1) and 189b(2) of the EC Treaty, the proposal for a European Parliament and Council decision amending Decision No 1254/96/EC laying down a series of guidelines for trans-European energy networks.

At the sitting of 9 October 1998 the President of Parliament announced that he had referred this proposal to the Committee on Research, Technological Development and Energy as the committee responsible.

The Committee on Research, Technological Development and Energy appointed Mr Adam rapporteur at its meeting of 28 October 1998.

It considered the Commission proposal and the draft report at its meetings of 26 January 1999 and 23 February 1999.

At the latter meeting it adopted the draft legislative resolution unanimously.

The following were present for the vote: Scapagnini, chairman; Adam, vice-chairman and rapporteur; Lange, vice-chairman; Argyros, Bloch von Blottnitz, Camisón Asensio (for Malerba), Chichester, Dimitrakopoulos (for Rovsing), Estevan Bolea, Gomolka (for W.G. van Velzen), Graenitz (for Rothe), Haug (for McAvan), Hudghton (for Weber), Linkohr, McNally, Mombaur, Plooij-van Gorsel, Rapkay (for Desama pursuant to Rule 138(2)), Robles Piquer (for MatikainenKallström), Soulier, Stockmann, Tannert and Trakatellis (for Ferber pursuant to Rule 138(2)).

The report was tabled on 25 February 1999.

The deadline for tabling amendments will be indicated in the draft agenda for the relevant partsession.

B EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

I. INTRODUCTION

The importance of the trans-European networks as an instrument of market integration and economic and social cohesion was recognised in the inclusion in the Treaty of European Union of a Title IX devoted to them.

Following a submission of a proposal by the Commission in late 1995, the Council and the European Parliament were able to adopt, on 29 June 1996; a Decision laying down a series of guidelines on trans-European energy networks. This contains the provisions defining the scope, objectives, priorities and main lines of action of the Community as regards projects for the transmission of electricity, the supply and transport of natural gas, with the aim of meeting the following criteria:

for electricity:

a) the linking of isolated electricity networks to the interconnected European systems,

b) the development of interconnections between Member States,

c) the development of internal connections required for the achievement of interconnections between Member States,

d) the development of interconnections with the third countries of Europe and the Mediterranean Basin contributing to improve the reliability and the security of supply of the Community"s electricity networks,

and for gas:

e) the introduction of natural gas into new regions,

f) the linking of isolated gas networks to the interconnected European networks, including the necessary reinforcement of the existing networks, together with the interconnection of separate natural gas networks,

g) the increasing of capacity for the reception and storage of LNG so as to meet demand, together with the diversification of sources and routes for conveying natural gas,

h) the increasing of transport capacity (pipelines) required to meet demand, together with the diversification of sources and delivery routes for natural gas,

The original guidelines, adopted in June 1996 as explained above contain a list of projects of common interest for the trans-European energy networks. These original guidelines have been modified on one occasion, by European Council and Parliament Decision N˚ 1047/97 of 29 May 1997. This modification, like the present proposal, took the form of an updating of the list of projects of common interest. The purpose of the 1997 updating was, among other things, to take account of the enlargement of the Community to include Sweden, Finland and Austria and also to further the interconnection of the electricity systems of Central Europe and the European Union and to facilitate the importation of natural gas from third countries, in line with market developments. On this occasion the Commission"s proposal was adopted by the Council and the European Parliament without modification.

II. COMMENTS

As reasons for the updating of the list of projects of common interest which is now proposed; the Commission cites the rapid development of the market, especially the natural gas market, and the need for diversified sources of supply and for increased storage capacity in order to serve it, and the need to pursue the setting-up of interconnected energy networks on the scale of the European continent.

In the first half of 1998, Commission officials consulted experts from the Member States, Community electricity and gas companies and the bodies representing those companies with a view to adding to the information available on trans-European network projects in the energy sector.

On the basis of these data, the Commission selected a total of 19 projects for addition to the list of projects of common interest. Four of these are already on the list of projects of common interest. For these four projects, the Commission proposes to change their description; the new description will better match the scope of each project, and the various options for its implementation. Three were already included in the Commission proposal laying down a series of guidelines, and are being proposed again because they have been further developed since then. Twelve are new projects which have emerged as a priority and have quickly matured over the in the last two-years. All these projects are considered to meet the criteria listed above which are applicable to them, and all are considered to have reached a suitable state of preparation for addition to the list. The Committee on Research, Technological Development and Energy agrees with this list and proposes two further additions, concerning Greece and Spain.

In approving and slightly extending the list proposed by the Commission, the Committee would like to remind the Commission of the external dimension of trans-European networks, i.e. to assess their implications for the specification of eligible projects of mutual interest, especially if the Commission attempts, at a later stage, to launch further legislative proposals which would lay down guidelines for pan-European electricity and natural gas networks. More specifically, the Committee calls on the Commission to present a strategy paper on the technical measures that would be required to open the European electricity grid to supplies from the former Soviet Union member states and what political implications arise from considerations of the environmental compatibility of these power stations. Although oil pipelines are not part of the trans-European energy networks the Commission should also put forward an anticipatory assessment of future problems of energysupply security that may arise from the fact that Asian countries such as the People's Republic of China are ever more interested in participating in the exploitation of Central Asian (e.g. Caspian) oil reserves.
